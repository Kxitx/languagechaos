Some irregular patterns which occur often:

Example Verb
Conjugation
verb stem ends in s, ß, x or z
heißen (to be called)
The s in the 2. Person Singular disappears: du heißt instead of *du heißst
verb stem ends in t, d, m or n (except for if it ends in the consonant clusters lm, ln, rm or rn)
atmen (to breathe)
2. Person Singular, 3. Person Singular & 2 Person Plural get an additional e to make it pronouncable: du atmest, er atmet, ihr atmet instead of *du atmst, er atmt, iht atmt
verbs which do not end in -en
lächeln (to smile)
Forms which normally get an -en just get a -n, and in the 1. Person Singular one e disappears: ich lächle, wir lächeln, sie lächeln instead of *ich lächele, wir lächelen, sie lächelen
vowel change e -> i (not all verbs do this!)
helfen (to help), essen (to eat), geben (to give)
2. & 3. Person Singular have a vowel change: du hilft, er hilft instead of *du helfst, er helft
vowel change e -> ie (not all verbs do this!)
sehen (to see), lesen (to read)
2. & 3. Person Singular have a vowel change: du siehst, er sieht instead of *du sehst, er seht
vowel change a -> ä & au -> äu (not all verbs do this!)
schlafen (to sleep), fahren (to drive), laufen (to walk)
2. & 3. Person Singular have a vowel change: du schläfst, er schläft instead of *du schlafst, er schlaft
